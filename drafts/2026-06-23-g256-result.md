## osoi5 g256 coarser-quant — negative result

Re-grouped osoi5's int4 body **g128 → g256** (halve the bf16 scale-byte overhead, a
prompt-invariant decode-bandwidth lever) and served it on the verified **506.74** hayai stack
unchanged: head-prune 16k→12k, kenyan-duma drafter (MTP K=7), sliding-window 192,
split-KV-verify / FA-sliding / ONEGRAPH / fused-sparse-argmax. Benched on `a10g-small`, 128
prompts × 512 tokens, single-stream. Job `6a39afd0c612b71be2578618`.

**Result: regresses on BOTH axes.**

- **tps 461.01** (vs 506.74 at g128). The kenyan-duma drafter is tuned to the g128 target
  argmaxes; the coarser g256 body shifts the target greedy tokens, so draft acceptance
  collapses — accept ratio **≈0.66** (461 output / 698 total tok/s) vs g128's **≈0.82**. The
  acceptance loss dominates any body-GEMM byte saving → net **slower**.
- **ppl 2.5628** (cap ~2.42). Coarser g256 scales raise body ppl **+0.17** over g128's 2.394 —
  far more than the hoped ~+0.02. Over the cap → **invalid**.

GPTQ-calibrated g256 would not rescue it: calibration lowers ppl only ~0.1 (→ ~2.45, still
borderline-invalid) and does nothing for the drafter-acceptance collapse.

**Conclusion: g256 coarser-quant does not beat 506 — it regresses both speed and validity.**
This upgrades [`docs/ROADMAP.md`](../docs/ROADMAP.md)'s "g256 (PPL-only)" entry from a *theoretical*
dead-end to an *empirically benched* one. Logged so the field does not re-try it. (Aux: the
regroup must copy `chat_template.jinja` or the head-pruned serve dir fails ChatTemplateResolution
at warmup.) Posted to the board as `status: negative` (archived, not plotted).
