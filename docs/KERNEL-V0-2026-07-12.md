# Kernel lane close-out: our own int4 GEMV vs Marlin on the A10G (2026-07-12)

We finally did the one never-done thing in the kernel lane: **wrote our own CUDA int4-g128 GEMV and
raced it against Marlin on the real challenge GPU.** Paid A10G jobs (JohnP1, ~$0.25 total, 4 fail-fast
iterations). Verdict: **Marlin wins 3.2–3.5×, and is itself at 86–90% of measured silicon peak — the
kernel lane is closed with first-party data.**

## Method (every past measurement mistake designed out)

- **Correctness before timing:** both kernels evaluated against fp32 references of their own logical
  weights (Marlin's grid recovered from its `marlin_quantize`; ours packed from the same integers);
  a shape that fails accuracy is never timed.
- **Determinism:** our kernel uses fixed-order strided accumulation + warp-shuffle tree reduction,
  no atomics (the greedy-identity requirement).
- **Graph-captured timing only**, with an **empty-graph assert** (output must change on replay — this
  caught a real instrumentation lie: a stale-stream capture produced a physically impossible
  2 µs/1200%-roofline result on run 3, rejected by the physics gate before it could be reported).
- **Physics gate:** any timing implying >105% of the *measured* copy peak is an error, not a result.

## Results (A10G, measured copy peak 481 GB/s)

| shape | Marlin | ours T128 | ours T256 | ours/Marlin |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2560×10240 (gate/up) | 31.3 µs · **89.8%** roof | 107.1 µs · 26.2% | 110.2 µs · 25.5% | 3.42× |
| 10240×2560 (down) | 32.1 µs · **87.7%** | 101.6 µs · 27.7% | 105.2 µs · 26.7% | 3.17× |
| 2048×16384 | 42.0 µs · **85.7%** | 138.8 µs · 25.9% | 147.4 µs · 24.4% | 3.31× |

Correctness: ours rel 1.70–1.77e-4 ≈ Marlin's 1.72–1.78e-4 (both vs fp32 reference). The kernel is
*right*; it is just slow — one-row-per-block with ~26% roofline is memory-level-parallelism-starved,
and closing 26%→90% would mean rebuilding Marlin's tile/async-copy/pipelining machinery to, at best,
tie it.

## The headline correction

The June 3080 spike measured Marlin at 66–78% of that card's copy peak and framed a "~13pp gap" as
possible headroom. **On the target A10G the gap is ≤10–14pp against measured peak (86–90% roofline)**
— i.e. the headroom mostly does not exist on the hardware that matters. This also bounds the untested
GemLite to the same ≤10% ceiling; it is retired without a run.

## Verdict

- **Kernel lane: CLOSED, first-party.** Correct hand-written GEMV, 3.2–3.5× behind; Marlin is
  near-silicon-optimal at M=1 on sm_86/A10G.
- Rules note for the record: custom kernels are fully legal (the verified frontier is built of them);
  the binding constraints are determinism + greedy-token identity + same-weights-both-paths, all of
  which our harness enforced.
